# WHO Essential Medicines and Health Information — India Context

## Fever Management

A fever is a body temperature above 38°C (100.4°F). It is the body's natural response to infection.

General management:
- Rest and drink plenty of fluids (water, ORS, coconut water, fruit juices)
- Paracetamol (acetaminophen) 500-1000mg every 6-8 hours for adults (maximum 4g/day)
- For children: paracetamol 10-15 mg/kg every 4-6 hours (syrup form preferred)
- Sponge the body with lukewarm (not cold) water if temperature exceeds 39°C
- Avoid aspirin in children under 12 years (risk of Reye's syndrome)

Seek immediate medical care if:
- Fever exceeds 39.5°C (103°F) in adults or 38°C in infants under 3 months
- Fever lasts more than 3 days
- Accompanied by severe headache, stiff neck, rash, confusion, or difficulty breathing
- No improvement after 48 hours of treatment

## Diabetes Management

Type 2 diabetes affects over 77 million Indians. Key management principles:

Diet:
- Limit refined carbohydrates: white rice, white bread, sugar, maida
- Increase fiber: brown rice, whole wheat, dal, vegetables
- Eat at regular intervals — avoid skipping meals
- Portions matter: use the plate method (half vegetables, quarter protein, quarter carbs)
- Monitor glycemic index of foods

Exercise:
- 150 minutes of moderate aerobic exercise per week (brisk walking, swimming, cycling)
- Resistance training 2-3 times per week
- Avoid prolonged sitting — move every 30 minutes

Monitoring:
- Check blood sugar as directed by doctor (typically fasting and post-meal)
- Target HbA1c < 7% for most adults
- Get annual eye, kidney, and foot exams
- Monitor blood pressure — target < 130/80 mmHg

## Maternal and Child Health

Antenatal care (ANC) recommendations:
- Minimum 4 ANC visits during pregnancy (WHO recommends 8)
- Iron-folic acid supplementation from early pregnancy: 1 tablet daily
- Tetanus toxoid vaccination: 2 doses
- Institutional delivery strongly recommended

Danger signs in pregnancy requiring immediate care:
- Severe headache with visual disturbances
- Severe abdominal pain
- Vaginal bleeding
- High fever
- Swelling of hands/face
- Decreased fetal movement

## Tuberculosis (TB)

India accounts for 26% of global TB cases. TB is curable with proper treatment.

Symptoms of TB:
- Persistent cough for 2+ weeks
- Blood in sputum
- Fever, especially evenings
- Night sweats
- Unexplained weight loss
- Loss of appetite

Treatment:
- DOTS (Directly Observed Treatment, Short-course) available free at all government health centers
- Treatment duration: 6 months minimum for drug-sensitive TB
- NEVER stop treatment early — incomplete treatment causes drug resistance
- Ni-kshay portal provides support and nutritional incentives

Prevention:
- BCG vaccine at birth
- Adequate ventilation
- Cover mouth when coughing/sneezing
- Early diagnosis and treatment of infected persons

## Mental Health

Common mental health conditions and first steps:

Depression:
- Persistent sadness, loss of interest, fatigue, sleep changes
- Help available: Vandrevala Foundation helpline 1860-2662-345 (24/7)
- iCall helpline: 9152987821

Anxiety:
- Excessive worry, restlessness, physical tension
- Breathing exercises: 4-7-8 technique (inhale 4s, hold 7s, exhale 8s)
- Avoid caffeine, alcohol, and irregular sleep

Emergency mental health (suicidal thoughts):
- iCall: 9152987821
- Vandrevala Foundation: 1860-2662-345
- NIMHANS: 080-46110007
- Speak to a trusted person immediately

## AYUSH Integration

Government of India promotes integrated medicine:
- Ayurveda, Yoga & Naturopathy, Unani, Siddha, Homeopathy (AYUSH)
- AYUSH treatments can complement but should not replace evidence-based care for serious conditions
- Always inform all your doctors about all medicines you are taking (AYUSH + allopathic)
- Some herbal medicines interact with prescription drugs
